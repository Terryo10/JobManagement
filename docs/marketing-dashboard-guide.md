# Marketing Dashboard Guide

> **URL:** `yoursite.com/marketing`
> **Brand:** Household Media — Marketing
> **Colour:** Rose / Pink

This guide walks you through every part of the Marketing dashboard. It is designed for the marketing team who manage leads, proposals, events, research, and business development.

---

## Logging In

1. Go to `yoursite.com/marketing/login`.
2. Enter your **email** and **password**.
3. Click **Sign in**.

After you sign in you land on the Marketing Dashboard home page.

## Dashboard Home Page Widgets

When you log in, you see these widgets:

### Announcements Widget
Shows the latest company announcement. Click to view all.

### Marketing Stats Overview (Top Row)
Three stat cards at the top:

| Card | What it Shows |
|---|---|
| **Active Leads** | Total number of leads with status New or In Progress. Click to jump to the leads list filtered to new leads. |
| **Converted This Month** | How many leads were successfully converted to clients this month. Click to see all converted leads. |
| **Open Proposals** | Number of proposals in Draft or Submitted status. Also shows the total dollar value of those proposals. Click to see submitted proposals. |

### Lead Pipeline Chart
A bar chart showing the number of leads in each stage:
- **New** (blue) — Just added, not yet contacted
- **In Progress** (amber) — Currently being worked
- **Converted** (green) — Successfully became a client
- **Lost** (red) — Did not work out

This gives you a quick picture of your pipeline health.

### Marketing Calendar
A full calendar showing your tasks, events, and deadlines. Switch between Month, Week, and List views.

### Upcoming Follow-ups (Next 7 Days)
A table showing all leads that have a follow-up date in the next 7 days. Columns:
- **Contact Name** — Who you need to follow up with
- **Company Name** — Their company
- **Status** — New (blue) or In Progress (yellow)
- **Follow-up Date** — When the follow-up is due. Today's date shows in orange. Future dates show in blue.
- **Assigned To** — Who is responsible for this lead

Click any row to jump straight to that lead's edit page.

### Recent Proposals Widget
Shows the latest proposals with their status and value.

### Upcoming Events Widget
Shows the next networking events coming up.

## Leads — Detailed Guide

Go to **Pipeline → Leads** in the sidebar. This is where you manage your sales pipeline.

### Lead Table Columns
| Column | What it Shows |
|---|---|
| **Contact Name** | The person at the potential client company |
| **Company Name** | Their company name |
| **Status** | New (blue), In Progress (yellow), Converted (green), Lost (red) |
| **Source** | How you found this lead (e.g., Referral, Website, Event) |
| **Assigned To** | Which team member is handling this lead |
| **Follow-up Date** | When the next contact should happen. Shows in red if overdue. |

### Filters
Click the funnel icon:
- **Status** — New, In Progress, Converted, Lost
- **Trashed** — Show deleted leads

### Creating a Lead
1. Click **New Lead**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Client** — If this lead is for an existing client, pick them from the dropdown.
   - **Contact Name** — Required. The person you are talking to.
   - **Contact Email** — Their email address.
   - **Contact Phone** — Their phone number.
   - **Company Name** — Their company.
   - **Source** — Where the lead came from (e.g., "Referral", "Trade Show", "Website").
   - **Status** — Starts as New. Change as you make progress.
   - **Assigned To** — Who on the team is handling this lead.
   - **Follow-up Date** — When to next get in touch.
   - **Notes** — Any information about the lead.
   - **Lost Reason** — If the lead is lost, explain why (for future learning).
3. Save.

### Lead Status Flow
```
New → In Progress → Converted (success!)
                   ↘ Lost
```

When a lead converts:
- If they are linked to an existing client, you can start creating work orders for that client.
- If they are new, first create a Client record, then link the lead.

## Communications — Detailed Guide

Go to **Pipeline → Communications** in the sidebar.

This is your contact log. Every time you call, email, or meet with a lead, log it here.

### Creating a Communication Entry
1. Click **Create**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Lead** — Which lead this communication is with.
   - **Channel** — Phone, Email, Meeting, WhatsApp, etc.
   - **Date** — When the contact happened.
   - **Notes** — What was discussed.
   - **Follow-up Date** — When to contact them next.
3. Save.

This builds a timeline of all interactions, so anyone on the team can pick up where you left off.

---

## Proposals — Detailed Guide

Go to **Pipeline → Proposals** in the sidebar.

### Proposal Status Flow
```
Draft → Submitted → Accepted (success!)
                   ↘ Rejected
```

### Creating a Proposal
1. Click **New Proposal**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Title** — What the proposal is for.
   - **Client** — Which client or prospect.
   - **Value** — The dollar value of the proposal.
   - **Status** — Draft, Submitted, Accepted, or Rejected.
   - **Description** — The full proposal details.
   - **Valid Until** — How long the offer stands.
3. Save.

### What to Do When a Proposal is Accepted
1. Change the status to **Accepted**.
2. Create a **Work Order** for the job.
3. If needed, create a **Quotation** (go to Finance → or ask the accounting team) with formal pricing.

## Market Research

Go to **Strategy → Market Research** in the sidebar.

Use this to store research findings that support your marketing decisions. Each entry has:
- **Title** — What the research is about.
- **Category** — Industry, Competitor, Market Trend, etc.
- **Description** — Full details.
- **Key Findings** — The main takeaways.
- **Source** — Where the data came from.
- **Date** — When the research was done.

---

## Networking Events

Go to **Strategy → Networking Events** in the sidebar.

Track all events where your team represents the company. Each event has:
- **Name** — Event title.
- **Date** — When it is.
- **Location** — Where it is.
- **Description** — What the event is about.
- **Attendees** — Who from your team is going.
- **Outcomes** — What came out of it (new leads, partnerships, etc.).
- **Follow-up Actions** — What needs to happen after the event.

## Requisitions

Go to **Finance → Requisitions** in the sidebar.

The process is the same as on the Staff dashboard:
1. Click **New Requisition**.
2. Fill in what you need the money for, the amount, link to a work order, add attachments.
3. Set status to "Pending Finance Approval" and save.
4. Track your requests in the table — see if they are awaiting finance, approved, or rejected.
5. Download a PDF of any requisition.

## Tips

- **Check the Upcoming Follow-ups widget daily** — It shows leads you need to contact in the next 7 days. Do not let follow-up dates pass without action.
- **Log every communication** — Even a quick phone call. This creates a history that helps the whole team and avoids repeating conversations.
- **Use the Lead Pipeline chart** — If most leads are stuck in "New" status, it means you need to start reaching out. If most are in "In Progress", you are on the right track.
- **Convert leads quickly** — The longer a lead sits without contact, the more likely it is to go cold.
- **Link proposals to clients** — This connects the business development pipeline to the client record, giving everyone visibility.
- **Use Market Research** — Before pitching to a new client, check if there is research about their industry or competitors.
- **Red on follow-up dates** means the date has passed — reach out as soon as possible.
- **Stat card numbers are clickable** — Click them to jump to the filtered list of records.
